Larry "Bucketman" Hunt

Better known as the “Bucketman,” Larry Hunt is perhaps the best known street performer in San Francisco. Using whatever is readily available - cans, plastic buckets, water jugs, milk crates - Larry’s signature beats can be heard throughout downtown. If timed right, you can also catch him perform fire-eating acts with his drumsticks.

Raised in Kansas, Larry started learning drums at age three. Hunt wound up penniless in Berkeley in 1983 and began playing drums on the Cal campus, sleeping in frat houses and student co-op basements, doing what he could to get by. On a good day, he’ll play for nine hours, collecting up to $60 in his tip jar, supplementing his Social Security check, and paying for a subsidized room in the city’s Tenderloin district.
